Mountains are large, elevated landforms that rise significantly above the surrounding terrain. They are often formed by tectonic plate movements, where plates collide and push the Earth's surface upwards. Mountains can be found on every continent and in various climates, with some like the Himalayas being the highest mountain range in the world. Here's a more detailed look at mountains: Formation: Tectonic Plates: Most mountains are formed when tectonic plates collide, causing the Earth's crust to fold and uplift. Volcanoes: Some mountains are created by volcanic eruptions, where lava piles up and cools over time, forming a volcanic cone.
